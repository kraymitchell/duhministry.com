a common sense alternative to religion

DUH Ministry is a non-denominational organization promoting the belief that we are all children of the same universe.

Our common sense approach is easy to grasp for all ages and allows you to live a happier lifestyle while spreading the joy to those around you.

DUH Minisitry was created by ULC Ordained Minister @kraymitchell.

Concepts

The following concepts are just that, concepts for a better life. While we hope that you embrace them, you are under no obligation to do so.

We encourage you to share our concepts with others if you feel that they are something you can get behind.

Don't be a douche:

Seriously, it’s not going to impress anybody. The old adage “Treat others as you wish to be treated” is the best way to describe this concept. Very simple to follow.

Your beliefs are your own:

You can choose to believe in whatever you want, but that does not mean others need to as well. It's great when we find like minded individuals, but having differences of opinion is part of human nature. Don't fight over trivial differences and instead try finding common grounds to bond.

No worship required:

The Universe is a tangible and limitless entity, of which we are some of it’s children. How you choose to acknowledge everything the universe has to offer is truly up to you. You are not required to pray or worship, you just need to believe!

Do more that makes you happy:

Life is too short to be wasted on things that don’t make you happy. If that means finding a new job, or removing people in your life, your happiness awaits you, don’t keep it at bay.

Pay it forward:

It's an old concept that still merits attention. If somebody does something nice for you, no matter how small, pass it on to somebody else. You never know what could change a persons entire state of mind.

It’s the little things:

No matter what you are doing, don’t overlook the little things. From doing something that’s not your job to help others to going a tiny bit further to make an experience that much better, it’s the little things that count.
